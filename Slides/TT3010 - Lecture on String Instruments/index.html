Sound Radiation of musical Instruments

The directivity pattern of a musical instrument might:

  • have one or several strong directions
  • depend FREQUENCY of sound
  • be OMNIDIRECTIONAL for Low Frequencies

The sound radiation from a Musical Instrument is MUCH MORE COMPLICATED than from a LOUDSPEAKER because the Musical instruments have more COMPLICATED DIRECTIVITY FUNCTIONS.

As for loudspeakers, directivity INCREASES with FREQUENCY, and the RADIATION MIGHT be OMNIDIRECTIONAL for LOW freq. (But not all instruemnts have OMNI. for low freq! because the instruments MIGHT act as a DIPOLE, if parts of the vibrating surface is moving OUTWARDS at the same time as other parts move INWARDS.

Room Acoustics:

$$L_p = L_W + 10log(\frac{Q}{4\pi r^2} + \frac{4}{A})$$
$L_p =$ sound pressure level, in dB
$L_w =$ sound power level, in dB
$Q =$ directivity factor (DF) of the sound source
$r =$ distance from the sound source, in m
$A =$ Total absorpotion area, in $m^2$
IN Room Acoustics lectures you certainly saw this formula which is widely used to calculate the Sound PRESSURE level from the sound POWER level. (Example of temperature and Heater!)
So, in this formula we can observe that the DIRECTIVITY factor DIRECTLY affects the BALANCE between the DIRECT sound and the REVERBERATION.
The relationship between direct sound and reverberation affects our PERCEPTION of CLARITY, how EASY for our ears is to hear DETAILS of music (Ex: church --> LONG REV.--> SLOW MUSIC).
Then, the music instruments' directivity affect HOW HIGH CLARITY the listener can EXPERIENCE.
  • Instru. with HIGH DIRECTIVITY can REACH OUT STRONG direct sound
  • Instrument CLOSE to a WALL with STRONG directivity migh cause EARLY REFLECTION and an effect of CHANGE of the DIRECTION fo the sound SOURCE!

Vibrations of a Bowed string

Stick-slip action of bow on string:
The string appears ot vibrate back and forth smoothly between 2 CURVED BOUNDARIES, like a FREE string in its FUNDAMENTAL MODE.

But, the string is actually NEARLY STRAIGHT with a SHARP BEND at ONE POINT --> it resembles the initial shape of the PLUCKED string!

The string is CARRIED ALONG by the bow, then DETACHES and MOVES rapidly back until it is caught by the BOW again.

Vibrations of a Bowed string

- CONSTANT SPEED of the BOW!

- b) the BEND has reached the BRIDGE and will REFLECT BACK BOWN the string until (f) where it reaches the NUT

- The stick and slip of the string against the moving bow are DETERMINED by the FORCE OF FRICTION between the HORSEHAIR and the STRING.

- Slipping begins when the BEND arrives from the NUT and ends when it arrives from the BIRDGE.
- The time required for 1 ROUND TRIP depends only on the string LENGTH and the WAVE VELOCITY (depends on TENSION and MASS) and the vibration FREQ. remains the same.

- Note that here there is ONE BEND and for the PLUCKED STRING there were 2!

- The envelop is 2 parabolas with AMPLITUDE proportional to the BOW VELOCITY. It also INCREASES when the string is BOWED NEAR to an end.

So now, let's just see an example of sound AMPLIFICATION through a body. Here is the tunig fork attached to a amplification box. You have surely tried it before, but the tuning fork does not sound if one DOES NOT TRANSMIT the virbations of the fork SOMEWHERE!

String Instruments: VIBRATION PATTERNS

1- Modes inside the cavity: $A_0$, $A_1$, $A_2$,...
What it is a mode?
A mode is a VIBRATION PATTERN happening at a CERTAIN Frequency.
The dash line is what we call the NODES coinciding with the POINTS where there is NO MOVEMENT at all.

For instance, When the VIOLIN is EXCITED at 284Hz, we have the (0,0) mode (some times also refered as Helmholtz resonance) and what we can see from the plot is that the AIR is MOVING ALL EQUALIY.

For (0,1) mode, it is called the LONGITUDINAL mode and you may imagine the air molecules drawing a WAVE up and down on the LONGITUDINAL direction so that the CENTER of the VIOLIN is NOT moving.

For (1,0) mode, is the TRANSVERSE mode and the wave is UP and DOWN but now having in the TRASNVERSE direction.
Observation: the f-holes are SYMMETRIC! and thus, the sound will radiate in THIS CASE is OPPOSITE polarity. The sound contribution at 1077Hz might be very WEAK!!
